The first modern rose was a hybrid developed by Jean-Baptiste Guillot, combining a hybrid perpetual rose with a tea rose. The most popular modern rose types are: Hybrid Tea Roses: Known for their long, straight stems and pointed blooms, these hardy shrubs are favorites among gardeners. Grandifloras: These roses produce large, showy flowers. Floribundas: A delightful mix of hybrid tea and polyantha roses, they offer clusters of blooms. Old Garden Roses: These are the classic roses cultivated before 1867. They include: Gallica Roses: Ancient and richly scented, they bloom once a year. Damask Roses: Known for their intense fragrance, they also bloom once annually. Alba Roses: Elegant and hardy, they have pale pink or white blooms. Wild Roses: These grow naturally and include species like the dog rose (Rosa canina). Rose Characteristics: Stems: Roses have stems armed with prickles (commonly called thorns).
